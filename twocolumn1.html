About Me

My name is Hunter Callaway, and I am so glad you are here! I am originally from Birmingham, Alabama. I left home to attend Davidson College in North Carolina until graduating in 2022 and joining the intake team for the Criminal Records Program at Georgia Justice Project in Atlanta, Georgia. I worked there for a little under two years before leaving to start my studies at Harvard Law School, where I am currently a first-year student.

I have always loved writing, but I have also been quite shy about writing in public. While I wrote several articles and opinion pieces for my college newspaper, The Davidsonian, I have done little writing in public since graduation. This website is my attempt to change that. I have very strong opinions about the future of legal services, and it is time for me to put my money where my mouth is. I mostly focus on work at the intersection of technology and legal services, especially as it applies to closing the access to justice gap, but be prepared to read about subjects including, but not limited to: Science Fiction, The Sahel, Self-Help, Travel, and my quest for the best hand-pulled noodles in Boston.

Stay tuned for more updates! This project is a work in progress, but I am excited to build it out over time. Let me know if you have suggestions on how to improve the site, especially if you have any experience with webdev or graphic design!
