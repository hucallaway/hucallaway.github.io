NASA's Future Fleet: Navigating the Proposed 2026 Budget Cuts

The White House's proposed 2026 budget for NASA includes a dramatic 24% cut to funding, reducing the agency's budget to its smallest since 1961 when adjusted for inflation. The Science Mission Directorate faces the most severe reduction, with a proposed 47% cut that threatens to defund or cancel over 40 active and future missions. According to an article from Astronomy.com, this could be an "extinction-level event" for NASA's science programs.

This graphic from Astronomy.com visualizes the NASA missions at risk. An 'X' marks missions slated for cancellation, while a '!' highlights those facing cuts of 40% or more. Click the image to read the full analysis.

Defend Planetary Science

Many of our most successful science probes are at risk right as they enter the most cost-effective phase of their missions. The Planetary Society is leading a global petition to Congress to show universal support for NASA and its mission of scientific discovery. Anyone can add their name.
